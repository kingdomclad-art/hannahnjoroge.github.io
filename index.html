Background

Why operational experience
is the missing piece in AI

Most people trying to break into AI are coming from one direction: technical. They can build the system. What they often can't do is run an organisation, manage diverse teams across cultures, coordinate international programmes, or understand what actually breaks when a new system meets real-world operations.

I've spent over a decade doing exactly that — in schools, NGOs, government census projects, and international development organisations. I've managed budgets, led teams of enumerators in the field, coordinated with UN agencies, and built systems from scratch.

Now I'm deliberately adding AI operations skills to that foundation — because I believe the organisations that will benefit most from AI are the ones that have someone who understands both the technology and the human realities of deploying it responsibly.

My work is guided by one conviction: technology should amplify human potential, not replace it. Especially in organisations doing meaningful work.
